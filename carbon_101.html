Understanding Carbon Credits

What is a Carbon Credit?

A carbon credit is a certificate representing the reduction or removal of one metric tonne of carbon dioxide (tCO₂e) from the atmosphere. These are generated by projects like tree planting (sequestration) or renewable energy farms (avoidance) and are traded on our exchange.

The Lifecycle of a Credit

  1. Development: A climate project is designed under a methodology (VCS/Gold Standard).
  2. Verification: A 3rd-party auditor (VVB) verifies the actual emission reductions.
  3. Issuance: The registry issues unique serial numbers for the credits.
  4. Trading: Credits are listed on Sparc Energy and purchased by buyers.
  5. Retirement: The credit is permanently cancelled, and the offset is claimed by the buyer.

VCS vs. Gold Standard

Verra’s **Verified Carbon Standard (VCS)** is the world’s most widely used voluntary program. **Gold Standard** focused on high-development impact and additional Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Both are supported 100% on Sparc Energy.
